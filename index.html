Md Mohiuddin

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Economics at Texas Tech University (on the 2026–2027 academic job market). I work in macroeconomics with a focus on energy and climate risk.

My research studies how households, firms, and local economies adjust to large shocks. Current projects examine (i) oil-price shocks and consumption smoothing, (ii) wildfire risk, insurance withdrawal, and local labor-market dynamics, and (iii) AI-driven trade exposure and labor adjustment.

Job Market Paper: Oil Shocks, Consumption Smoothing, and Welfare Losses: A State-Dependent Test of the LCPIH. [Draft] · [Slides]

I combine local projections, BVAR, and quasi-experimental designs to identify dynamic responses to shocks and quantify welfare implications.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in conceptual clarity, analytical discipline, and real-world application. I begin with intuition, move to formal analysis, and then connect the concept to data or a current policy problem.

Whether teaching introductory macroeconomics or econometrics, I emphasize how households, firms, and policymakers respond to shocks, constraints, and incentives.
